CT6A2 - DATA VISUALIZATION

Pre-requisites: Basics of Probability and Statistics

Course Objective(s):

  1. To introduce students with fundamental concepts, approaches, and applications of data visualization
  2. To familiarize students with different visualization and analytic tools and techniques
  3. To develop skills and competencies required by the professionals

Data, Perception and Data Visualization, Graphical Excellence – Data Maps, Time-Series, Graphics of Space and Time, Graphical Integrity – Distortion in a Data Graphic, Design and Data Variation, Visual Area and Numerical Measure; Apply Visualization Tools for Today’s Digital Age

Data-Ink and Graphical Redesign – Data-Ink, Maximizing the Share of Data-Ink, Two Erasing Principles, Application of the Principles in Editing and Redesign; Data-Ink Maximization and Graphical Design – Redesign of Box Plot, Histograms, and Scatterplot, Data Density

Visualizing Amounts – Bar Plots, Grouped and Stacked Bars, Dot Plots and Heatmaps; Visualizing Distributions – Histograms and Density Plots, Q-Q Plots, Skewed Distributions; Visualizing Proportions – Pie Charts; Visualizing Time Series – Individual Time Series, Multiple Time Series, Time Series with Multiple Response Variables, Trends.

Mapping, Connections and correlations, Multivariate Analysis, Scatterplot Maps, Trees, Hierarchies, and Recursion, Networks and Graphs.

Introduction to Tableau – Adding Data Sources in Tableau, Creating Data Visualizations, Aggregate Functions, Advanced Analytics: Trends, Forecasts, Clusters, and other Statistical Tools; Python Data Visualization Libraries; Visual Analytics – Visual Analytics Process, Application and Technical Challenges, Applications and Case Studies.

Course Outcomes :

Students will be able to

  1. Understand and apply the fundamental design principles of data visualization
  2. Present data by applying different data visualization techniques for better communication
  3. Analyse data and communicate information through visual representation
