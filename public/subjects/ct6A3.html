CT6A3 - OPTIMIZATION TECHNIQUES

Course Objective(s):

  1. To understand the role of optimization techniques and its importance in engineering
  2. To introduce the concept of unconstrained optimization methods. 3
  3. To formulate and solve linear programming problems
  4. To explore and understand non-linear programming problems

UNIT I Introduction and Unconstrained optimization
Linear Transformations – Eigenvalues and Eigen Vectors – Quadratic forms – Matrix form – Concepts from Geometry – Hyperplanes – Convex sets – Calculus – Differentiation rules – level sets and gradients

Unit II Unconstrained optimization I
Conditions for local minimizers – One dimensional sarch methods – Golden Section Fibonacci – Secant Method – Gradient methods – Steepest descents – analysis – Newton’s method – Analysis – Levenerg – Marquardt modification – Conjugate Direction methods

Unit III Unconstrained Optimization II
Quasi-Newton methods - DFP – BFGS algorithm – Solving Linear Equations – Recursive least-squares – kaczmaraz’s algorithm - Unconstrained optimization and neural networks – Single neuro training – backpropagation algorithm – Global search – Simulated annealing - Particle Swarm optimization – Genetic algorithms

Unit IV Linear programming
Simple examples – Two-dimensional linear programs – Standard forms – Simplex method – Simplex algorithm – matrix form – two-phase simplex method - Non-simplex methods – Kachiyan’s method – affine scaling method – karmarkar’s method - Integer linear programming – Gomory Cuting-plane method

Unit V Non-linear Constrained optimization
Problems with equality constraints – Lagrange Condition – Second order conditions – minimizing quadratics subject to linear constraints Problems with inequality constraints – Karush-kuhn-tucker conditions – second order conditions – convex optimization problem – constrained optimization problem – lagrangian algorithms

Course Outcomes :

Students will be able to

  1. Suggest constrained or unconstrained optimization approach for solving real-time problems
  2. Formulate and solve linear programming problems (LPP)
  3. Evaluate Integer Programming Problems, Transportation and Assignment Problems.
  4. Formulate and suggest Non-linear programming solution to any real-time problems, Optimize any real-time problem using constraints.
