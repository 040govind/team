CT6B1 - Cognitive Computing

Course Objective(s):

  1. To understand basics of Cognition, Brain Functions and Relations to Computing.
  2. To study the architectures and functionalities of Cognitive Computing.
  3. To understand the principles of inference and DSS and their links to Cognitive Computing.
  4. To study and practice the significance of ML, NLP, Graphs in Cognitive Computing.
  5. To understand how cognitive computing applications are built and practiced.

Introduction: Cognitive science and cognitive Computing with AI, Cognitive Computing - Cognitive Psychology - Architecture of the Mind - Nature of Cognitive Psychology - Reverse Engineering the Brain - Brain Scalability - Neocortical Brain Features - Concept of a Basic Circuits – Large-Scale Cortical Simulations.-

Cognitive architecture – Cognitive processes – Cognitive Modelling Paradigms - Declarative / Logic based Computational cognitive modelling – connectionist models – Bayesian models. Introduction to Knowledge-Based AI – Human Cognition on AI – Cognitive Architectures.

Cognitive Computing With Inference and Decision Support Systems: Intelligent Decision making, Fuzzy Cognitive Maps, Learning algorithms: Non linear Hebbian Learning – Data driven NHL - Hybrid learning, Fuzzy Grey cognitive maps, Dynamic Random fuzzy cognitive Maps.

Cognitive Computing with Machine Learning: Machine learning Techniques for cognitive decision making – Hypothesis Generation and Scoring - Natural Language Processing - Representing Knowledge - Taxonomies and Ontologies - Deep Learning.

Case Studies: Cognitive Systems in health care – Cognitive Assistant for visually impaired – AI for cancer detection, Predictive Analytics - Text Analytics - Image Analytics -Speech Analytics – IBM Watson.

Course Outcomes :

Students will be able to

  1. Understand the principles of Cognition theory in Applications.
  2. Analyze various architectures and models used in Cognitive Computing.
  3. Design ML and Deep Learning Models for Cognitive Computing applications.
  4. Design framework using NLP, DSS and Inference in Cognitive Computing Applications.
