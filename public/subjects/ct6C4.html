CT6C4 - Data Privacy and Security

Pre-requisites: Basic knowledge of information systems and IT architectures.

Course Objective(s):

  1. To provides an in-depth understanding of data privacy and security in technology-enabled environments.
  2. To understand strategic and regulatory issues of data privacy and security in modern organizations.
  3. To develop a deep understanding of the design principles for data privacy and security.
  4. To understand technological solutions, methods, and practices for data protection in business organizations and peer to peer networks.

Fundamentals of Data Privacy & Security: Privacy in the Digital Age, Use of Online Services and Products, Personal Data Categories, Data Protection, Government Surveillance, Ethical Issues, Data Privacy Rights, Data Privacy Regulations and Initiatives, General Data Protection Regulation (GPDR), Data security, Challenges to Data Privacy and Security.

Data Privacy Mechanisms: Databases and Exploratory Data Analysis, Data Representation and Storage, Data Masking, Tokenization, Anonymization - Linkage and Re-Identification Attacks, k-anonymity, l-diversity, t-closeness, Implementing Anonymization, Personal Data Reidentification Risk Assessment, Anonymizing Complex Data, Data Fabrication, Privacy, and Anonymity in Mobile Environments.

Differential Privacy (DP): Formalism and Interpretation of DP, Fundamental DP Mechanisms and Properties, Interactive and Non-interactive DP, DP for Complex Data, Local Differential Privacy (LDP)

Data Security: Identity Theft, Phishing and Online Threats, Authentication and Authorization, Database Security, Software Vulnerabilities, Cyberattack Models, Planning, Designing, and Implementing IT Security, Cryptography, Network Security, Security Operation Tools and Techniques.

Security and Privacy in AI and Machine Learning (AI/ML): Machine Learning (ML) Background, Adversary Modeling in AI/ML, Poisoning, Evasion, and Backdoor Attacks, Test-Time Attacks: Model Inversion, Model Stealing, Membership, Inference, Adversarial Examples, Privacy-preserving Data Mining, Architectures and Algorithms for Privacy-preserving Machine Learning.

Course Outcomes :

Students will be able to

  1. Analyze the social and organizational implications of cyber risk in digital ecosystems.
  2. Select, design, and implement the most appropriate security controls to mitigate risk in business organizations.
  3. Apply data analytics tools and techniques to support decision making in incident management and security operations
  4. Design innovative solutions using data-driven analytics and deep learning for situational awareness in security operations.
